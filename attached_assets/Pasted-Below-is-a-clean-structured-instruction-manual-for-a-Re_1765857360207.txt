## SECTION 1: Psychological Self-Observation (CBT-Aligned)

### Title

Psychological self-observation
الملاحظة النفسية

### Core Principle (display text)

Ibn ʿArabī emphasizes awareness before action. Modern psychology confirms this: noticing thoughts, emotions, and bodily signals reduces reactivity and increases clarity.

### Content Rules

* Do not diagnose
* Do not label emotions as good/bad
* Use neutral language: “notice”, “observe”, “track”

### Suggested Guidance Copy

Encourage users to:

* notice mood shifts across the day
* identify recurring triggers (fatigue, conflict, overstimulation)
* observe thoughts without immediately see them as facts

### CBT Alignment (short note)

This mirrors cognitive-behavioral practice: awareness → interpretation → response.

### Journaling Prompts (rotating or static)

* What am I feeling right now?
* What thought is accompanying this feeling?
* Is this thought a fact, or an interpretation?
* What response would reflect adab in this moment?

## SECTION 2: Mood, Body & Somatic Awareness

### Title

Somatic and emotional awareness
الوعي الجسدي والعاطفي

### Core Principle

The body registers meaning before the intellect. Awareness of bodily signals helps regulate emotional states.

### Guidance Copy

Invite users to:

* notice physical sensations (tightness, heaviness, restlessness, calm)
* map emotions to body regions (chest, stomach, jaw, shoulders)
* track energy levels rather than forcing productivity

### Somatic Prompts

* Where do I feel this emotion in my body?
* Is my body signaling rest, movement, or stillness?
* What happens if I slow my breathing for one minute?

---

## SECTION 3: Cycles, Rhythms & Long-Term Patterns

### Title

Cycles and personal rhythms
الدورات والإيقاعات الشخصية

### Core Principle

Time is lived differently by each person. Patterns become visible only through review.

### Guidance Copy

Encourage tracking of:

* daily energy rhythms
* monthly emotional cycles
* seasonal shifts
* significant life events

### Inclusive Language Rule

Use **gender-neutral wording**, but explicitly acknowledge:

* hormonal cycles
* menstrual cycles
* mood variability related to physiology

Example phrasing:
“Some people experience monthly physiological rhythms that affect mood and energy. Tracking these patterns can increase self-compassion and planning clarity.”

### Reflection Prompts

* What patterns repeat each month?
* Which times support clarity? Which require gentleness?
* How does my body respond to stress over time?

## SECTION 4: Regulation & Restoration Practices

### Title

Regulation and restoration
التهدئة وإعادة التوازن

### Core Principle

Alignment with time requires a regulated nervous system.

### Categorize practices into 3 cards or lists:

#### A) Nervous System Calming

* slow breathing (long exhale)
* quiet walking
* gentle stretching
* body-based grounding

#### B) Water & Heat Therapies

* warm baths
* water immersion
* onsen-style soaking
* contrast showers (gentle)

#### C) Manual & Passive Relaxation

* lymphatic drainage
* Thai or gentle massage
* resting in low-light environments

### Language Rule

Avoid “fix”, “optimize”, “hack”.
Use “support”, “allow”, “restore”.

## SECTION 5: Cognitive & Environmental Hygiene

### Title

Cognitive and environmental clarity
صفاء ذهني وبيئي

### Core Principle

Excess stimulation disrupts perception of time and meaning.

### Guidance Copy

Encourage:

* reducing information intake during stress
* limiting multitasking
* simplifying environments

### Practical Suggestions

* digital quiet hours
* reduced notifications
* minimalist visual environments
* single-task focus

### Organization Tools (Neutral)

Mention categories, not brands:

* task lists
* reminders
* calendar blocking

Purpose:
To reduce cognitive load, not increase productivity pressure.

## SECTION 6: Support for Neurodiverse Users

### Title

For neurodiverse users
للمستخدمين ذوي الحساسية العصبية

### Tone Rule

Respectful, validating, non-medical.

### Guidance Copy

Acknowledge that some users may be more sensitive to:

* light
* sound
* visual clutter
* information density
* social intensity

### Practical Suggestions

* low-stimulus environments
* softer lighting
* noise reduction (ear protection)
* predictable routines
* fewer but deeper social interactions

### Design Instruction

If possible, allow:

* reduced animation
* simplified visual mode
* minimal color contrast
