## **Primary Works by Ibn ʿArabī**

### **1. al-Futūḥāt al-Makkiyya (The Meccan Openings)**

The central and most comprehensive source for Ibn ʿArabī’s cosmology, view of time, celestial symbolism, and spiritual psychology.

Recommended thematic chapters include:

* **Ch. 1–2**
  On the nature of knowledge, divine self-disclosure (tajallī), and the structure of existence.

* **Ch. 73 – The Spirits of the Hours**
  On planetary hours, angelic attendance of time, and spiritual adab in responding to moments.

* **Ch. 178 – Imagination (al-Khayāl)**
  Foundational for understanding how celestial meanings appear in the world and in the heart.

* **Ch. 195 – The Heavens and the Spirits Governing Them**
  On the seven heavens, planetary intelligences, and their role as mediators of meaning.

* **Ch. 198 – On Time, the Moon, and the Lunar Mansions**
  Core chapter for this app:

  * nature of time (waqt, dahr)
  * renewal of creation (khalq jadīd)
  * lunar mansions as stations of meaning
  * the Moon as mirror of divine decree

* **Ch. 390 – The Spirit of Time**
  On time as a living reality and its relation to divine presence.

* **Ch. 559 – From Divine Decree to Manifest Event**
  On the descent of meanings through cosmic levels into lived reality.

These chapters collectively ground the app’s approach to time as **symbolic, non-causal, and inwardly readable**.

---

### **2. Mawāqiʿ al-Nujūm (The Setting-Points of the Stars)**

A concise but profound work focused on the **spiritual journey through changing states**, using celestial imagery.

Key themes relevant to the app:

* movement through light and shadow
* alternation of expansion and contraction
* spiritual stages unfolding over a lifetime
* warnings against mistaking signs for guarantees

This text strongly informs the app’s emphasis on **awareness rather than prediction**.

---

### **3. Inshāʾ al-Dawāʾir (The Creation of the Circles)**

A short but foundational cosmological treatise.

Relevant topics:

* concentric structure of reality
* the human being as microcosm
* relationship between divine knowledge and created forms
* the heart as the meeting point of heaven and earth

This work underlies the app’s cosmological diagrams and “heart as mirror” principle.

---

## **Authoritative Secondary Scholarship**

### **4. Titus Burckhardt – *Mystical Astrology According to Ibn ʿArabī***

One of the most precise and respectful expositions of Ibn ʿArabī’s celestial symbolism.

Especially useful for:

* lunar mansions as qualitative time
* rejection of causal astrology
* distinction between symbol and mechanism

Burckhardt’s work closely adheres to Ibn ʿArabī’s metaphysical intent and avoids folk astrology.

---

### **5. William C. Chittick – *The Sufi Path of Knowledge***

The most reliable English-language guide to Ibn ʿArabī’s metaphysics.

Recommended chapters:

* sections on **tajallī (divine self-disclosure)**
* sections on **time, imagination, and the heart**
* discussion of **non-repetition of disclosure**

Chittick is essential for readers who want conceptual clarity without distortion.

---

### **6. William C. Chittick – *Imaginal Worlds***

Highly relevant for understanding:

* imagination as a real ontological realm
* how celestial meanings appear in perception
* why symbols are neither literal nor arbitrary

This work supports the app’s treatment of mansions and hours as **imaginal lenses**.

---

### **7. Henry Corbin – *Creative Imagination in the Sufism of Ibn ʿArabī***

A classic study of:

* imaginal perception
* unveiling and veiling
* spiritual vision versus fantasy

Corbin’s work helps users understand *how* meaning appears without reducing it to psychology or superstition.

---

## **Related Classical Context (Optional Deepening)**

### **8. Qur’anic foundations**

Especially verses frequently cited by Ibn ʿArabī:

* “Every day He is upon a task” (Qur’an 55:29)
* “We are closer to him than his jugular vein” (50:16)
* “God is the Light of the heavens and the earth” (24:35)

These verses underpin the concepts of renewal, presence, and symbolic disclosure.

---

## **How to Use These Sources**

These works are not manuals for prediction or control.
They are aids for **contemplation, self-knowledge, and adab with time**.

Readers are encouraged to approach them:

* slowly
* reflectively
* with attention to inner states rather than outcomes

---

### Closing note (app-appropriate)

The teachings referenced here describe **how meaning becomes readable in time**, not how to secure specific results. They invite attentiveness, humility, and presence rather than certainty.
